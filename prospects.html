TOP PROSPECTS 2023/2024



Nathan Roy

IOL - Minnesota University

Born and raised in Nevada, Mukwonago offensive lineman Nathan Roy and his family moved to Wisconsin during his sophomore year of high school. Arriving just in time for football season, Roy made an instant impact for the Indians. He played his first season on the defensive line due to the all senior offensive line that year not needing him. The next 2 seasons he dominated on the offensive line, receiving over 20 division 1 offers. To many people's surprise, he chose Minnesota over many more successfull programs.


Offers From: Minnesota, Iowa State, Penn State, Wisconsin, Iowa, Illinois, Nebraska, USC, Kansas, Purdue, Indiana, Syracuse, Miami, Michigan State, Tennessee, Ohio State, Kentucky, Oklahoma, UCLA, Florida





Garrett Sexton

OT - Penn State University

You might not believe this, but Penn State recruit Garrett Sexton started off as a quarterback. Garrett was still playing quarterback during his sophopmore year, standing at 6 foot 5, 195 pounds. Now as a senior, the 6 foot 7, 270 pound monster will be playing offensive line for Penn State. Arrowhead coach Matt Harris said that it wasn't until he saw Garrett playing basketball during his sophopmore year that he realized his full potential rested in the offensive line position.


Offers From: Penn State, Baylor, Duke, Illinois, Iowa, Iowa State, Kansas, Michigan State, Minnesota, Missouri, Northwestern, Oklahoma, Princeton, Purdue, Rutgers, Stanford, Syracuse, Tennessee, Wisconsin





Derek Jensen

OT - Wisconsin University

Derek Jensen was a player somewhat neglected by the old Wisconsin coaching staff. Even though Jensen had offers from other Big 10 schools, the Arrowhead star was completely ignored by the badgers. It looked as though he would be playing for Iowa, then Luke Fickell showed up. Jensen stated that it was a complete 180 with the new coaching staff, and decided to commit to Wisconsin.


Offers From: Wisconsin, Central Michigan, Duke, Illinois, Iowa, Iowa State, Kansas, Michigan State, Minnesota, Nebraska, Purdue, Syracuse, Virginia
